X-Ray Reflectometry — Thin-Film Metrology · solo report

Resolved a C/W multilayer on silicon by XRR (Cu-Kα, 1.54 Å) — critical-angle, Kiessig-fringe and Bragg-peak analysis with least-squares fitting. Extracted 60.4 ± 0.2 nm total thickness, a 3.2 nm bilayer period, electron density and surface roughness. When the vendor software (XOP) failed, I re-implemented the full reflectivity analysis in MATLAB to finish the work.

Featured Research — Amorphous-Silicon SPADs

M.Sc. thesis & project work: one of the few systematic studies of hydrogenated amorphous-silicon (a-Si:H) single-photon avalanche diodes — from device simulation through cleanroom fabrication to full electro-optical characterization, closing the loop between metrology, device performance and fabrication process parameters.

Almost all commercial SPADs use crystalline silicon in costly CMOS processes. Amorphous silicon can instead be deposited on arbitrary substrates at low temperature — enabling Thin-Film-on-ASIC (TFA) sensors with near-100% fill factor and electronics placed underneath the detector. The trade-off is a material with more defects and lower carrier mobility, and its single-photon behavior was largely uncharted.

I fabricated four TCO – p – i – n – TCO mesa devices (three a-Si:H, one a-Si:C:H) using two transparent-conductive-oxide anodes — AZO and ITO — then characterized each by I–V, temperature-dependent I–V, spectral response and dark-count measurements to isolate how i-layer thickness, doping and deposition vacuum govern performance.

Custom probe station with integrated hot plate, used for temperature-dependent I–V measurements.

⚛ Interactive — fire a photon at the diode

A single photon absorbed in the i-layer creates an electron–hole pair; the reverse-bias field accelerates the carriers until impact ionization multiplies them into a measurable avalanche. Click anywhere on the device to fire a photon — and watch for spontaneous dark counts, the noise source I measured as DCR.
